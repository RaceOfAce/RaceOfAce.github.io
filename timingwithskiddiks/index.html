OTHER THINGS TO DO AND NOT TO DO.

HOW TO SNAP KEYFRAMES

See those purple lines in the audio? If you actually read the timing guide, you'd know those are keyframes. Basically, it's when a scene changes. Key frame snapping is when you time a line to start/end at the same time as a keyframe. If your subs are still up after a scene change, you do something called a scene bleed. These are very, very bad. don't do these.

WHEN TO SNAP TO A KEYFRAME AT THE END OF A LINE

Normally, you'd add in more lead-in, but that would cause a bleed. If you drag the end time to a keyframe, it will automatically be snapped to it. If you want to double check, for whatever reason hit the third from last button here:
This button will go to the end of a line. Now click where the video is. If you press left and right, you'll framestep the video. Framestep one to the right. Scene should change on the video if it's timed correctly. The button before it will move to the beginning of a line. The next to buttons will set the start or end times to the current video frame respectively.

OMG! THERE'S A GAP BETWEEN THE KEYFRAME AND END TIME!

First, time it like you normally would, including lead-out. If you're <400ms from the keyframe, you're probably safe to snap. If it's more than that, use your best judgment. When all else fails, do it then see how it looks. Does it look awkward to have the subs stay up a long time? Does it look awkward if the subs end before the scene change? Do whatever you think is best.

WTF THERE'S LIKE 9001 SCENE CHANGES!

This happens during heavy action scenes or fades. Find where the keyframe legitmately ends and time it to that. In this case, the first kf since that's where the scene actually changes.

WHEN DO I NOT SNAP?

For fake keyframes. Those are similar to the above. For example, you may get a keyframe for something like a head tilt. You should time those normally. Once you get used to timing, you'll actually notice whether a keyframe is fake or not. When in doubt though, always check the video.

WHEN DO I SNAP TO THE BEGINNING OF A LINE?

Like the last part, but you generally don't want to have too much lead-in. I'd say adding no more than an additional 250ms.

WHAT IF THEY ARE TALKING AT THE SAME TIME?

Two things you can do:

  • {\an8} is the aegisub tag that moves subs to the top of the screen. Put background dialog up here. If it's over multiple lines, keep it consistent. For example, if it's a teacher talking in the background, make all of his lines \an8 and all the main dialog on the bottom.
  • Alternate style is making a copy of the current style, but with a different outline. The alt style should be on top of the main dialog.. The first line will be on the bottom, the next line will be on the top. Like this:
    • You generally use an alternate style for people saying the same/similar things at the same time. When in doubt, just use \an8.
